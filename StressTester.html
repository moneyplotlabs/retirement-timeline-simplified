Methodology: Runs 1,000 Monte Carlo iterations, sampling historical annual returns at random with replacement (bootstrap resampling). The net worth chart displays a percentile fan: p10/p25 as dashed lines (downside), p50 as the solid median. The asset growth bars show the single simulation run whose terminal balance lands closest to the 10th percentile outcome — a coherent real return sequence that produced a bad-but-realistic result, not a synthetic worst-case-every-year path. Success rate = share of runs ending above the legacy floor.

Data Attribution: All historical return series cover 1928–2024 (97 years) and are expressed as real (inflation-adjusted) annual returns.

US Equities (S&P 500) — nominal total returns (price + dividends) sourced from Aswath Damodaran, NYU Stern. CPI deflation applied using U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics data via BLS / Robert Shiller. Real arithmetic mean 8.8%, std dev 19.6%, geometric CAGR 6.9%.

60/40 Portfolio (60% S&P 500 + 40% 10-Year US Treasury Bond) — bond total returns (coupon + price appreciation) from FRED, St. Louis Fed as compiled by Damodaran; blended and deflated by CPI. Real arithmetic mean 5.9%, std dev 12.5%, geometric CAGR 5.1%.
