Bifurcated Growth Rates: This model supports independent growth rates for the working and retirement phases. By unlinking these, you can simulate a "glide path" transition from growth-oriented assets while working to more conservative, capital-preservation assets (e.g., bonds) in retirement. 3% for most time frames results in same retirement age as following 4% safe withdrawal rate rule.

Pure Math Framework: All figures are calculated in today's dollars (inflation-adjusted). The default 3.0% real growth rate means your investments grow by 3% on top of inflation (e.g., if inflation is 2%, your nominal return is 5%). This conservative baseline is structurally designed to mirror real-world safety nets like the 4% Rule.

While historic portfolios (e.g., a balanced 60/40 mix) average a long-term 5.7% real growth rate, they carry a 9.5% standard deviation. This "Sequence of Returns" risk means dropping the baseline to a smooth 3.0% mathematically simulates the volatility buffer needed to survive severe market downturns.

Data Attribution: Historical S&P 500 and US Treasury Bond returns are sourced from Aswath Damodaran's dataset at NYU Stern. Figures represent real annual returns adjusted for inflation via CPI.
