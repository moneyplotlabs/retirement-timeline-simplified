Pure Math Framework: All figures are calculated in today's dollars (inflation-adjusted). The default 3.0% real growth rate during accumulation means your investments grow by 3% on top of inflation (e.g., if inflation is 2%, your nominal return is 5%). This conservative baseline is structurally designed to mirror real-world safety nets like the 4% Rule.

While historic portfolios (e.g., a balanced mix of 60% stocks and 40% bonds) average a long-term 5.7% real growth rate (approximately 8.8% nominal minus 3.1% inflation), they carry a 9.5% standard deviation. This volatility creates a "Sequence of Returns" risk—if the stock market crashes right as you retire, a fixed 5.7% assumption fails. Dropping the baseline to a smooth 3.0% real growth rate during accumulation mathematically simulates that volatility buffer, ensuring your plan survives severe market downturns.
